Privacy Policy

Your Privacy Matters: At Connecticut Ave Dental Studio, we are committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal and health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information in compliance with federal and state privacy laws, including HIPAA.

We collect various types of information to provide you with quality dental care and to operate our practice effectively:

  • Name, address, phone number, and email address

  • Emergency contact information
  • Insurance information and billing details
  • Employment information when relevant for treatment

Health Information

  • Medical and dental history
  • Current medications and allergies
  • Treatment records and clinical notes
  • X-rays, photographs, and other diagnostic images
  • Treatment plans and progress notes
  • Billing and payment records

Website Information

  • IP address and browser information
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Information submitted through contact forms
  • Appointment requests and communications

How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

Treatment

  • Providing dental care and treatment
  • Coordinating care with other healthcare providers
  • Maintaining accurate treatment records
  • Following up on treatment outcomes

Payment

  • Processing insurance claims and payments
  • Billing for services rendered
  • Collecting outstanding balances
  • Financial assistance programs

Healthcare Operations

  • Quality assessment and improvement
  • Staff training and education
  • Business planning and development
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory requirements

Communications

  • Appointment reminders and scheduling
  • Treatment recommendations and follow-up care
  • Practice updates and important notices
  • Patient education materials

HIPAA Compliance

Connecticut Ave Dental Studio is fully committed to compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and other applicable privacy laws.

Protected Health Information (PHI)

We maintain strict safeguards for your Protected Health Information and limit access to:

  • Healthcare providers directly involved in your care
  • Administrative staff who need the information for treatment, payment, or operations
  • Business associates who have signed HIPAA-compliant agreements
  • Individuals you have authorized in writing

Minimum Necessary Standard

We follow the "minimum necessary" standard, meaning we only use or disclose the minimum amount of information necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

Notice of Privacy Practices

You will receive our complete Notice of Privacy Practices, which provides detailed information about how we may use and disclose your health information. This notice is available at our office and upon request.

Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your personal and health information:

Physical Safeguards

  • Secure office facilities with controlled access
  • Locked filing cabinets for paper records
  • Secure disposal of confidential information
  • Workstation security and access controls

Technical Safeguards

  • Encrypted electronic health records systems
  • Secure network connections and firewalls
  • Regular software updates and security patches
  • Automatic logoff procedures for computer systems
  • Regular data backups with secure storage

Administrative Safeguards

  • Regular HIPAA training for all staff members
  • Written policies and procedures for privacy protection
  • Background checks for employees with access to PHI
  • Incident response procedures for potential breaches
  • Regular security risk assessments

Your Rights

As our patient, you have important rights regarding your personal and health information:

Right to Access

You have the right to request and receive copies of your health records. We will provide access within 30 days of your request, though we may charge reasonable fees for copying costs.

Right to Amendment

You may request amendments to your health information if you believe it is incorrect or incomplete. We will review all requests and make appropriate corrections when warranted.

Right to Restrict Use

You may request restrictions on how we use or disclose your health information. While we will consider all requests, we are not required to agree to all restrictions.

Right to Alternative Communications

You may request that we communicate with you about your health information in a specific way or at a specific location to protect your privacy.

Right to an Accounting

You may request an accounting of disclosures of your health information that we have made for purposes other than treatment, payment, or healthcare operations.

Right to File a Complaint

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with our office or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.

Information Sharing

We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Treatment Coordination: With other healthcare providers involved in your care
  • Insurance Processing: With your insurance company for claims processing
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or regulatory agencies
  • Public Health: For public health activities as required by law
  • Emergency Situations: To prevent serious harm to you or others
  • Business Associates: With vendors who provide services to our practice under HIPAA-compliant agreements

We will never sell your personal information to third parties or use it for marketing purposes without your explicit consent.

Website Privacy
