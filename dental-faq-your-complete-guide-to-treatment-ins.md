# Dental FAQ: Treatment & Insurance Guide

Quick answers to common dental questions about procedures, insurance coverage, and emergency care.

## Common Dental Procedures

**What's included in a routine cleaning?**
Plaque removal, tartar scaling, tooth polishing, and fluoride treatment. Takes 45-60 minutes with oral examination.

**How long do dental implants last?**
20-30 years with proper care. 95% success rate when placed by experienced professionals.

**What does a root canal involve?**
Removes infected pulp, cleans tooth interior, and seals the space. 1-2 visits with 90% success rate.

## Insurance Coverage

**Does insurance cover cosmetic procedures?**
Most plans exclude cosmetics like veneers ($800-2,500) or whitening ($300-800). Functional restorations may receive partial coverage.

**What's my annual maximum benefit?**
Most plans provide $1,000-$2,000 annual coverage with 50-80% coinsurance for major procedures.

**Are orthodontics covered?**
Adult braces typically aren't covered. Children may receive 50% coverage up to $1,000-$2,000 lifetime maximum.

## Treatment Planning

**How often should I visit the dentist?**
Every 6 months for cleanings and exams. High-risk patients need quarterly visits. Regular care prevents 80% of emergencies.

**What about dental anxiety?**
We offer nitrous oxide, oral sedation, and IV options. 90% of anxious patients complete treatment successfully.

## Emergency Care

**When should I seek emergency treatment?**
Severe pain lasting 24+ hours, knocked-out teeth, facial swelling, or uncontrolled bleeding require immediate care.

**Can knocked-out teeth be saved?**
Yes, if treated within 60 minutes. Keep tooth moist in milk or saliva. Success drops to 10% after 2 hours.
