# How to Choose the Right Toothbrush for Your Dental Health

Choosing the right toothbrush is key to keeping your teeth healthy. With so many options available, knowing what to look for makes all the difference.

## Bristle Type Matters Most

Soft bristles are best for most people. They clean effectively without damaging your gums or tooth enamel.

Medium and hard bristles can cause gum recession over time. Our dental team recommends soft bristles for daily use.

## Manual vs. Electric Toothbrushes

Both manual and electric toothbrushes work well when used correctly. Electric toothbrushes make it easier to clean thoroughly.

Key features to consider:

- Timer functions for proper brushing time
- Pressure sensors to prevent over-brushing
- Multiple cleaning modes
- Replaceable brush heads

## Size and Shape Guidelines

Your toothbrush head should fit comfortably in your mouth. A smaller head reaches back teeth more easily.

The handle should feel secure in your grip. This helps you control the brush better during cleaning.

## When to Replace Your Toothbrush

Replace your toothbrush every three months. Worn bristles don't clean as well as new ones.

Change your toothbrush after being sick to avoid reinfection.
