## When to Seek Emergency Dental Care

Not every dental issue requires immediate attention, but recognizing true emergencies can save your tooth—and prevent serious complications.

### True Dental Emergencies

These conditions require immediate treatment:

- Severe tooth pain that prevents sleep or normal activities
- Knocked-out permanent teeth
- Loose or displaced teeth from trauma
- Broken teeth with sharp edges cutting your mouth
- Facial swelling affecting your ability to swallow
- Bleeding that won't stop after 10 minutes
- Lost fillings or crowns causing severe pain

### Urgent But Non-Emergency Issues

These can wait for regular office hours:

- Mild toothache manageable with over-the-counter pain relievers
- Small chips without pain
- Lost fillings without discomfort
- Food stuck between teeth
- Minor gum irritation

## Managing Dental Emergencies Before Treatment

While waiting for professional care, these steps provide relief:

**For severe pain:** Take over-the-counter pain relievers as directed. Apply cold compress for 15-20 minutes to reduce swelling.

**For knocked-out teeth:** Handle by the crown only. Rinse gently with water if dirty. Try reinserting the tooth or store in milk. Seek treatment within 30 minutes for best results.

**For broken teeth:** Rinse with warm water. Save pieces if possible. Apply gauze to control bleeding and protect your tongue from sharp edges.
