## Recognizing True Dental Emergencies

Not every dental issue requires emergency care, but certain situations demand immediate attention from our experienced team.

### Severe Pain and Swelling

Intense tooth pain that doesn't respond to over-the-counter medication signals a serious problem. Facial swelling, especially around the jaw or neck, can indicate a dangerous infection requiring immediate treatment.

### Knocked-Out or Broken Teeth

Trauma from sports, accidents, or falls can damage teeth beyond repair. Quick action within the first hour significantly improves the chances of saving a knocked-out tooth.

### Uncontrolled Bleeding

Bleeding from the gums or mouth that won't stop after applying pressure needs professional evaluation. This could indicate injury to soft tissues or underlying dental issues.

## Common Emergency Dental Situations

Our emergency care team regularly treats various urgent dental conditions affecting Washington DC residents.

### Abscessed Teeth

Dental abscesses cause severe pain and can spread infection throughout your body. These require immediate treatment to prevent serious complications.

### Lost Fillings or Crowns

While not always painful, exposed tooth structure becomes vulnerable to further damage and infection. Prompt treatment protects your oral health.

### Invisalign Treatment Emergencies

Patients receiving Invisalign treatment in Washington DC may experience urgent aligner problems. Broken aligners can cause discomfort and disrupt treatment progress. Lost aligners leave teeth unprotected and can cause shifting. Sharp edges from damaged aligners may cut your cheeks or gums, requiring immediate attention to prevent infection.

## What to Do During a Dental Emergency

Taking the right steps before reaching our office can make a significant difference in treatment outcomes.

### For Knocked-Out Teeth

- Handle the tooth by the crown, never the root
- Rinse gently with water if dirty
- Try to reinsert the tooth in its socket
- If reinsertion isn't possible, store in milk or saliva
- Seek treatment within one hour

### For Severe Pain

- Rinse your mouth with warm salt water
- Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling
- Take over-the-counter pain medication as directed
- Avoid placing aspirin directly on the gums

While emergencies happen, many can be prevented through proper oral care and precautions.
